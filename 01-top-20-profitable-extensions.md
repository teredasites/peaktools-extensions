## TIER 1: The Giants ($50M+/yr)

### 1. Honey (PayPal Honey)
- **What**: Auto-finds and applies coupon codes at checkout
- **Revenue**: ~$100M/yr; acquired by PayPal for **$4 BILLION** (2020)
- **Users**: 17-20 million at acquisition
- **Team**: 2 founders -> 350 employees
- **Time to profit**: Struggled 2.5 years, then Reddit leak drove 900K users
- **Key insight**: Solved universal frustration (finding coupons) with zero friction. Made money from retailers, not users.
- **Room for competitor?**: Limited. PayPal's distribution is massive. But Honey was exposed for hijacking affiliate cookies, creating distrust.

### 2. Grammarly
- **What**: AI writing assistant across all text inputs in browser
- **Revenue**: $700M ARR (2025). Was $43.5M (2019) -> $250M (2023) -> $400M (2024)
- **Users**: 40M daily active
- **Team**: 3 founders, bootstrapped 9 YEARS before raising $110M Series A
- **Time to profit**: Profitable from Day 1
- **Key insight**: Chrome extension turned niche student tool into everyday utility embedded in every text field

### 3. Loom
- **What**: Screen recording and async video messaging
- **Revenue**: $50M ARR; acquired by Atlassian for **$975M** (2023)
- **Users**: 18M users, 1.1B minutes watched
- **Time to profit**: 3,000 users within 24hrs of Product Hunt launch
- **Key insight**: Every video shared = new potential user. Viral loop built into the product.

## TIER 2: Bootstrapped Cash Machines ($1M-$20M/yr)

### 4. Wappalyzer
- **What**: Identifies tech stack of any website
- **Revenue**: **$18M (2024)** -- $0 raised, 3 employees
- **Users**: 3M extension users
- **Time to profit**: 16 years of compounding
- **Key insight**: Free extension is top-of-funnel. Real money is B2B data/lead sales. $6M revenue per employee.

### 5. GMass
- **What**: Turns Gmail into mass email/outreach platform
- **Revenue**: **$8.6M/yr (2024)** -- fully bootstrapped
- **Users**: 500K+ signups
- **Team**: **SOLO FOUNDER** (Ajay Goel, Chicago)
- **Time to profit**: Months. Built as side project.
- **Key insight**: Built ON TOP of Gmail. Zero learning curve. Status quo improvement, not disruption.

### 6. Hunter.io
- **What**: Finds professional email addresses for any domain
- **Revenue**: **$8.4M/yr** -- bootstrapped
- **Users**: 4M+
- **Key insight**: Free extension = lead magnet. Revenue from API access and bulk searches.

### 7. vidIQ
- **What**: YouTube analytics/SEO tools overlaid on YouTube
- **Revenue**: **$8.9M (2024)** -- nearly doubled YoY
- **Users**: 20M+ creators
- **Key insight**: Official YouTube-certified partner. Data where creators already work.

### 8. Momentum Dashboard
- **What**: Replaces new tab with inspiring dashboard
- **Revenue**: **$1M+ ARR**
- **Users**: 3M+
- **Key insight**: New tabs seen 10-50x/day = prime real estate. 0 to 100K users in 4 months via Reddit.

## Key Patterns

1. **Freemium is king** -- every top earner offers a free tier
2. **Extension = funnel, not product** -- real money often in web platform/API behind it
3. **Build where people already are** -- inside Gmail, YouTube, Twitter, not separate apps
4. **Niche beats broad** -- Closet Tools (Poshmark only) > generic productivity
5. **Solo devs can hit $100K-$1M/yr** -- CSS Scan, GoFullPage, Closet Tools prove it
6. **83% profit margins** -- near-zero marginal costs
7. **One perfect feature > many mediocre features** -- GoFullPage proves $120K/yr on ONE button
8. **Platform risk is real** -- BlackMagic died when Twitter raised API prices
